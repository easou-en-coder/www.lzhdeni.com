How to use these case studies

These pages are not meant to be copied blindly. They are meant to help readers understand which parts of a system are stable, which parts depend on context, and which tradeoffs appear when a team tries to implement change.

Read for patterns
Look for recurring problems: unclear ownership, tool sprawl, weak handoffs, or planning drift.
Compare contexts
A system that works for a founder may not work for a freelancer or a documentation-heavy team.
Reuse selectively
Adapt the underlying structure, not the exact surface ritual.
Link back to principles
Use the case studies with Foundations, Systems, and Team Work pages for deeper transfer.
